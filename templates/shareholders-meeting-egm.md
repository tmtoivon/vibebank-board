LEGAL NOTES:

Extraordinary General Meeting per Finnish Companies Act (OYL):

When EGM required or requested (OYL 5:4):
- Board decides to convene EGM
- Auditor requests EGM in writing
- Shareholders holding ≥10% of shares request EGM in writing for specific purpose

Notice (OYL 5:19):
- Same as AGM: earliest 2 months, latest 1 week before meeting
- Notice must contain agenda items
- Shareholders cannot add items to EGM agenda (unlike AGM where 10%+ can request items per OYL 5:16)

Common EGM purposes:
- Share issues (especially directed issues requiring ⅔ majority)
- Option plan approvals and authorisations
- Amendments to articles of association
- Board changes outside AGM cycle
- Urgent dividend distributions
- Approvals of significant transactions
- Capital increases/decreases

Quorum:
- No statutory quorum for Oy unless specified in articles
- For certain decisions (e.g., directed share issue), qualified majority of ⅔ of votes and shares represented required

Voting:
- Simple majority unless OYL or articles require qualified majority
- Qualified majority (⅔) required for:
  - Directed share issue (OYL 9:4)
  - Amendment of articles (OYL 5:27)
  - Share redemption (OYL 15:5)
  - Merger decisions (various provisions)

PRH filing:
- Same requirements as AGM decisions
