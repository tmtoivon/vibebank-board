USAGE NOTES:

This snippet is for SHARE ISSUES (osakeanti) per OYL Chapter 9.

Types of share issues:
1. Ordinary issue: Open to all shareholders, pre-emptive rights apply
2. Directed issue: To specific subscribers, requires waiver of pre-emptive rights
3. Rights issue: Offered pro rata to existing shareholders
4. Bonus issue: From reserves, no payment

Key legal requirements:

Ordinary issue:
- Shareholders have pre-emptive rights (etuoikeus) per OYL 9:3
- Simple majority at GM (or board under authorisation)

Directed issue (suunnattu anti):
- Requires WAIVER of pre-emptive rights
- Requires "weighty financial reason" (painava taloudellinen syy) per OYL 9:4
- Requires 2/3 of votes cast AND 2/3 of shares represented at GM
- Cannot be done by board even with authorisation (GM decision only for waiver)

Pricing:
- Must not be below nominal value (if par value shares)
- Should reflect fair market value
- Below-market pricing may trigger tax issues for subscribers

Authorisation to Board:
- GM can authorise board to issue shares (OYL 9:2)
- Max 5 years authorisation period
- Authorisation can specify max number of shares
- Board CANNOT waive pre-emptive rights unless GM authorisation explicitly permits

PRH filing:
- Required within 2 months after subscription period closes
- Supporting docs: GM/board resolution, subscription forms, proof of payment
- Shares become valid upon PRH registration

Common contexts:
- Fundraising rounds (directed to investors)
- Employee incentives (directed to employees)
- Debt conversion (directed to creditors)
- Strategic partnerships (directed to partner)
