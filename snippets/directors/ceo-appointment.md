### Duties and Responsibilities

Pursuant to OYL 6:17, the Managing Director's duties include:

1. **Day-to-day management** (juoksevat asiat):
   - Manage daily operations of the company
   - Execute decisions of the Board of Directors
   - Implement company strategy as directed by the Board

2. **Information duties**:
   - Ensure accounting is lawful and asset management properly organised (OYL 6:17)
   - Provide Board with information needed for Board's duties
   - Prepare materials for Board meetings

3. **Representation**:
   - {{REPRESENTATION_AUTHORITY}}

<!-- Representation examples:
- "Represent the company within the scope of day-to-day management"
- "Represent the company jointly with {{CO-SIGNATORY}}"
- "As determined by signatory rights (see separate resolution)"
-->

4. **Compliance**:
   - Ensure compliance with law, Articles of Association, and Board directives
   - Manage company within scope delegated by Board

5. **Reporting**:
   - Report regularly to Board on company performance and significant matters
   - Attend Board meetings (unless Board decides otherwise)
   - Prepare financial reports and budgets

USAGE NOTES:

This snippet is for APPOINTING a Managing Director (toimitusjohtaja, CEO).

Legal framework (OYL Chapter 6):

Requirement (OYL 6:12):
- Not mandatory for Oy (optional)
- Articles may require MD
- Good practice for active companies to have MD

Appointment authority (OYL 6:12):
- Board of Directors appoints MD (not GM)
- Board determines MD's duties and authority
- Board can remove MD at any time

Eligibility:
- Natural person (not company)
- No residency requirement
- Can be board member (but not recommended for governance)
- Can be employee or external

Duties (OYL 6:17):
- Manage "day-to-day" (juoksevat asiat) operations
- Ensure lawful accounting and proper asset management
- Execute Board decisions
- Provide Board with necessary information
- Not authorised to make decisions "unusual or far-reaching" without Board approval

Relationship with Board:
- MD subordinate to Board
- Board supervises MD
- Board can instruct MD
- MD typically attends Board meetings (but no vote unless also director)

MD as Board member:
- Legally permitted
- Not recommended (governance separation)
- Common in small companies
- If MD is director: should not chair Board (conflict)
- MD-director should recuse from decisions about own employment/performance

Delegation of authority:
- Board determines what MD can decide alone
- Typical thresholds: contract value, capex, hiring level
- "Day-to-day" vs "unusual" is fact-dependent
- Clear delegation avoids disputes and ensures efficiency

Employment relationship:
- MD typically has employment contract
- Subject to employment law protections
- Notice periods, termination rules apply
- Can also be engaged as consultant (less common)
- Remuneration determined by Board

Signatory rights:
- MD often has signatory rights (alone or jointly)
- Separate from management authority
- Must be filed with PRH
- See signatory-rights.md snippet

Removal:
- Board can remove MD at any time (OYL 6:13)
- No reason required
- Employment contract may provide compensation for termination
- File removal with PRH

PRH filing:
- File YTJ2 within 2 months of appointment
- Same information as for directors
- Update when MD changes
- Include signatory rights if applicable

Common structures:
1. **Small Oy**: No MD, Board manages directly
2. **Medium Oy**: MD handles operations, Board sets strategy
3. **Larger Oy**: MD + management team, Board supervises
4. **Startup**: Founder often serves as both director and MD

Remuneration:
- Determined by Board (not GM like director fees)
- Can include salary, bonus, equity, benefits
- Equity often via option grants (see option snippets)
- Benchmark against market rates

Best practices:
- Written employment contract (clarity)
- Clear delegation of authority (efficiency)
- Separate MD and Chair roles (governance)
- Regular reporting to Board (oversight)
- Annual performance review (accountability)
- Market-competitive remuneration (retention)

Handover from predecessor:
- If replacing previous MD: plan transition
- Knowledge transfer
- Customer/partner introductions
- Access to systems
- Outstanding matters completion
